How Our Online Eye Test Works

Simple, step-by-step process designed for accuracy and ease of use

1

Screen Calibration

Use a standard credit card to calibrate your screen size, ensuring accurate letter sizing for precise visual acuity measurement across different devices and screen sizes.

2

Environment Setup

Position yourself 60cm from your screen in good lighting. Remove glasses unless you normally wear them, and ensure no glare on your screen for optimal testing conditions.

3

Choose Starting Eye

Select which eye you'd like to test first. You'll cover the other eye completely while testing, ensuring individual assessment of each eye's visual performance.

4

Letter Direction Test

Identify the direction of the letter "E" as it appears in 8 progressively smaller sizes. Each level represents different visual acuity measurements from 20/200 to 20/20.

5

Switch Eyes

After completing all levels with your first eye, switch to test your second eye using the same 8-level progression to ensure comprehensive bilateral assessment.

6

Receive Results

Get instant analysis showing accuracy percentage, level reached for each eye, and overall vision assessment with professional recommendations for next steps.

Online Eye Test Rules & Guidelines

Follow these important guidelines to ensure accurate and reliable vision screening results

Proper Distance

Maintain 60cm (arm's length) from screen

  • Use the credit card calibration feature
  • Sit upright with good posture
  • Keep your head level and straight
  • Avoid leaning forward or backward
üí°

Lighting Conditions

Ensure optimal lighting environment

  • Use bright, even room lighting
  • Avoid screen glare and reflections
  • Set screen brightness to maximum
  • Face away from windows or light sources

Eye Covering Technique

Properly cover the non-testing eye

  • Use your hand or a piece of paper
  • Cover completely without pressing on the eye
  • Keep the testing eye open and focused
  • Don't peek with the covered eye

Glasses & Contacts

Follow your normal vision correction routine

  • Wear glasses if you normally use them
  • Keep contact lenses in if regularly worn
  • Clean glasses/contacts before testing
  • Test without correction for baseline measurement

Understanding Your Eye Test Scores

Learn how our scoring system works and what your results mean for your vision health

Scoring Methodology

How We Calculate Your Score

  • Accuracy Percentage: Number of correct responses divided by total responses
  • Level Reached: Smallest letter size you could accurately identify
  • Visual Acuity Estimate: Approximate Snellen fraction (20/20, 20/40, etc.)
  • Individual Eye Performance: Separate analysis for each eye tested
